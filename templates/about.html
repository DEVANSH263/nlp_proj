About HateShield

Understanding the system, the problem, and the approach.

What is HateShield?

HateShield is a web-based multilingual hate speech detection system designed to classify text as HOF (Hate/Offensive) or NOT (Non-Offensive).

It specifically addresses the challenge of code-mixed language — text that mixes Hindi written in Roman script (Hinglish) with English — which is pervasive on Indian social media platforms but poorly handled by standard English-only hate speech classifiers.

The Problem

Online hate speech causes real harm. The problem is especially acute in South Asia where:

  • Users frequently write in Hinglish — a mix of Hindi (in Roman script) and English.
  • The same word can be spelled 10+ different ways phonetically (e.g. pagal, paagal, pagal, pagl).
  • Existing NLP models trained on standard English fail to detect hate in such inputs.
  • There is no standardised Unicode for Romanized Hindi, causing vocabulary explosion.

HateShield directly addresses this by incorporating a transliteration normalization step before classification.

Our Approach
1. Text Preprocessing

Lowercase conversion, URL removal, @mention stripping, punctuation removal, and whitespace normalisation.

2. Transliteration Normalization (Key Innovation)

Using fuzzy string matching (threshold ≥ 85%), each token is compared against a curated Hinglish–English dictionary. Matching tokens are replaced with their English equivalents before classification.

3. Classification

A trained ML classifier (TF-IDF vectorizer + sklearn model) or a keyword-based heuristic predicts HOF or NOT along with a confidence score.

4. Reporting & Analysis

Every prediction is stored per user. Downloadable PDF reports include distribution charts, normalization insights, and session-level conclusions.
